Competency and Values Framework (CVF) Questions

What is the Competency and Values Framework (CVF) for Policing?

The CVF (2024, Level 1) outlines three values (Courage, Respect & Empathy, Public Service) and six competencies (e.g., We Support & Inspire, We Are Emotionally Aware, We Analyse Critically) to guide policing behavior and performance. It sets expectations for professional conduct, ensuring officers act ethically and effectively in diverse situations.

What are the three CVF values and their key behaviors?

  • Courage: Do the right thing in challenging situations, even at personal risk. Example: Speak up against unethical behavior or support a struggling colleague.
  • Respect & Empathy: Treat people with dignity, show compassion, and value diversity. Example: Listen non-judgmentally and adapt communication to individual needs.
  • Public Service: Show selfless commitment to community safety and wellbeing. Example: Prioritize public needs, considering diverse backgrounds and histories.

Which CVF competencies are assessed in the interview, and what are their key behaviors?

  • We Support & Inspire: Foster a positive team environment by helping and motivating others. Example: Share knowledge with new officers and encourage inclusive participation.
  • We Are Emotionally Aware: Recognize and manage emotions to build rapport and reduce stress. Example: Show sensitivity when supporting upset victims.
  • We Analyse Critically: Use evidence and logic for informed decisions. Example: Assess incident data to identify patterns and act decisively.

How would you demonstrate Courage in a policing scenario? (Use STAR method)

Situation: Witnessed a colleague using excessive force during an arrest.

Task: Ensure ethical standards are upheld.

Action: Calmly intervened, reminded the colleague of proportionate force guidelines, and reported the incident to a supervisor per protocol.

Result: The situation de-escalated, the colleague was retrained, and team trust in ethical standards increased.

How would you show Respect & Empathy when dealing with a distressed victim? (Use STAR method)

Situation: Responded to a domestic abuse call where the victim was visibly upset.

Task: Provide reassurance and support.

Action: Listened actively without interrupting, used calm and clear communication, and signposted the victim to local support services.

Result: The victim felt heard, trusted the police, and engaged with support services.

How do you apply We Analyse Critically in a policing context? (Use STAR method)

Situation: Investigated a series of local burglaries with limited leads.

Task: Identify suspects and prevent further crimes.

Action: Reviewed CCTV footage, cross-referenced witness statements, and identified a pattern in entry methods, leading to targeted patrols.

Result: A suspect was apprehended, and burglary rates in the area decreased.

Police Constable Role and Responsibilities Questions

What are the primary duties of a UK Police Constable?

Protect the public, prevent crime, and maintain order through:

  • Responding to 999/101 calls to ensure safety and secure scenes.
  • Patrolling, conducting stop-and-searches, and gathering intelligence for crime prevention.
  • Investigating crimes by collecting evidence, interviewing suspects, and preparing prosecution files.
  • Making arrests and processing detainees with proportionate force.
  • Supporting victims and communities by offering reassurance and building partnerships.
  • Performing court duties, paperwork, and training.

How does a Police Constableâ€™s role vary daily?

No two days are identical, involving a mix of high-pressure tasks (e.g., responding to domestic incidents or pursuits) and proactive community work (e.g., school visits or neighborhood meetings), requiring adaptability and quick decision-making.

Essential Qualities and Skills Questions

  • Analytical Thinking: Assess risks and make evidence-based decisions (links to CVF We Analyse Critically).

  • Physical and Ethical Fitness: Maintain readiness and uphold integrity (links to SYP Integrity).
